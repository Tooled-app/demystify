## This Week's Signal

**DeepSeek V4: The Open-Source Empire Strikes Back**

On April 24, 2026, DeepSeek dropped a bomb. V4 Preview: 1.6 trillion parameters, 1 million token context window, open-source weights, and performance that rivals the best closed-source models from OpenAI and Anthropic.

This isn't an incremental upgrade. DeepSeek reasserts itself as the most important force in open-source AI since Llama. And it attacks the three things enterprise customers care about most: cost, context length, and agentic reliability.

**V4-Pro**: 1.6T total parameters, 49B active. Claims it trails only Gemini-3.1-Pro overall.
**V4-Flash**: 284B total, 13B active. Smaller, faster, cheaper — with reasoning capabilities "closely approaching" the Pro version.

Both support 1M tokens of context by default. Both are open-source. Both ship with "Thinking" and "Non-Thinking" modes. And both are available via API today.

The strategic implications are profound. If an open-source model can match GPT-5.5 at a fraction of the inference cost, the proprietary API moat weakens. Enterprises can self-host. Startups can avoid vendor lock-in. Governments can deploy sovereign AI without sending data to US companies.

The catch? DeepSeek is sunsetting its old models by July 24 — forcing all API customers to migrate. It's aggressive product management. And the geopolitical discount means some Western enterprises will avoid DeepSeek on principle, regardless of quality.

**GPT-5.5 and the Superapp Gambit**

OpenAI didn't release a model. They released an ecosystem.

GPT-5.5 ships across ChatGPT, Codex, and "Thinking" mode. 400K context window. Computer use capabilities. 85% of OpenAI's own company uses Codex weekly. The benchmarks are validation stickers — the real signal is in the use cases.

The superapp thesis is clear: OpenAI is building the layer between humans and digital work. Not the application layer. The infrastructure layer. The thing everything else runs on. The endpoint isn't a better ChatGPT. It's a world where "using AI" is as meaningful a phrase as "using electricity."

The vertical integration risk is equally clear. The more useful GPT-5.5 becomes, the more dependent you become on OpenAI's entire stack. Model, inference, interface, memory, tools, safety — all gated by one vendor.
