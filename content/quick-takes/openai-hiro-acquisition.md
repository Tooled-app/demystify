OpenAI just bought Hiro Finance. On the surface, it's an acqui-hire for financial math expertise. In reality, it's a strategic play for the "Money Agent" vertical. 

Personal finance is the ultimate test of AI reliability—one hallucinated decimal point and the user loses real money. By absorbing Hiro's specialized training for financial accuracy, OpenAI is signaling that it wants to move from "helping you budget" to "managing your wealth."

The kicker? Hiro's founder is an OpenClaw power user. OpenAI isn't just buying tech; they're buying into the agent-native culture.
