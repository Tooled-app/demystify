---
title: "Chrome 'Skills': Google's Attempt to Normalize Prompt Engineering"
date: "2026-04-15"
category: "Quick Take"
tags: ["Google", "Chrome", "Gemini", "UX"]
---

Google adding "Skills" (saved prompts) to Chrome is a tacit admission that prompting is still too hard for the average user. 

By turning a prompt into a "Skill," Google is trying to bridge the gap between "AI power user" and "casual browser." It's a UX win, but it also highlights the fragility of the current AI interaction model: we are still just wrapping text strings in a browser. 

The real move isn't the "Skills" library; it's the integration. When your browser knows exactly how you want your recipes summarized across every site you visit, the browser becomes the agent.
