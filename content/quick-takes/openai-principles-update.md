# OpenAI Rewrote Its Charter for a World That Already Changed

**Published: April 27, 2026**

OpenAI updated its core principles for the first time since 2018, and the document reads less like a research lab's manifesto and more like a Big Tech playbook.

Sam Altman published five new principles on Sunday. The most striking changes:

**1. AGI dropped from 12 mentions to 2**
The 2018 charter centered on artificial general intelligence as the organizing mission. The 2026 version talks about "AI across the board." The north star hasn't moved, but the language has.

**2. The "stop competing" clause is gone**
The original charter included a remarkable commitment: if a value-aligned project got close to AGI first, OpenAI would "stop competing with and start assisting this project." That clause is gone. The new document says nothing about stepping aside. Instead, it acknowledges OpenAI is "a much larger force in the world" and suggests there may be future trade-offs between empowerment and resilience.

**3. "Our primary fiduciary duty is to humanity" is gone**
The 2018 charter's most quoted line is absent. The new principles talk about democratic decision-making and universal prosperity, but the language shifts from binding commitments ("we will," "we commit") to suggestions for the broader tech ecosystem.

**Why now?**
OpenAI is worth roughly $850 billion in private markets. Anthropic recently hit a $1 trillion valuation on secondary markets. The competition is fierce, the stakes are existential, and the original charter's idealism looks increasingly like a liability.

**What it means:**
This isn't a scandal. It's an honest admission that the 2018 framework was written by a nonprofit research lab with $1 billion in pledges, and the 2026 version is written by a company that processes billions of API calls per day. The principles are catching up to reality.

But the shift from "we commit" to "we acknowledge" matters. OpenAI is no longer promising to behave a certain way. It's offering guidelines for how the industry should think about AI's impact. That's a different kind of document entirely.

The charter used to be a constraint. Now it's a conversation starter.
