**The news:** Business Insider reports Anthropic has surged to a trillion-dollar valuation on secondary markets. The company that didn't exist eight years ago is now worth more than Bitcoin, more than Tesla at its peak, and roughly equivalent to the GDP of Switzerland.

**Reality check:**

Secondary market valuations are fantasy football for venture capital. They reflect what a small number of willing buyers will pay for illiquid shares, not what the company would fetch in an actual IPO or acquisition. They're also self-fulfilling: high secondary prices create FOMO, which drives higher prices.

But even as a fantasy, $1 trillion is staggering.

**Why this matters:**

1. **The comparison set is absurd.** At $1T, Anthropic would be worth more than Meta, more than Berkshire Hathaway, more than every bank on Wall Street combined. Its annual revenue is reportedly under $5 billion. That's a 200x revenue multiple. Even by AI standards, that's generous.

2. **The Pentagon drama adds spice.** Anthropic is simultaneously fighting the Department of Defense (which labeled it a supply-chain risk) while the NSA reportedly uses its Mythos cybersecurity model anyway. Trump says a deal is "possible." A trillion-dollar company in a national security tug-of-war is unprecedented.

3. **The Claude Code pricing crisis contradicts the valuation.** Anthropic is actively testing the removal of Claude Code from its $20 Pro plan because usage has made the economics unsustainable. A trillion-dollar company whose most popular product is hemorrhaging money on its cheapest tier has a business model problem, not a valuation problem.

4. **Secondary markets are not primary markets.** When SpaceX reportedly considered buying Cursor for $60 billion, that was a strategic acquisition price based on actual synergies. Anthropic's $1T secondary price is based on scarcity, hype, and the belief that someone else will pay more later.

**The bottom line:**

Anthropic is a genuine technical leader. Claude is arguably the best consumer AI product. Claude Code is the best coding agent. But $1 trillion is not a valuation -- it's a sentiment indicator. It says more about how desperate investors are to own a piece of AI's future than it does about Anthropic's present. When the correction comes, it won't be gentle.
