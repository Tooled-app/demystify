**The news:** OpenAI launched Workspace Agents for ChatGPT Teams and Enterprise users. These aren't chatbots you prompt once and forget. They're autonomous workers that run scheduled tasks, maintain persistent memory across sessions, and operate independently in the cloud.

**What they actually do:**

- Monitor and report on product feedback automatically
- Run scheduled research and data gathering tasks
- Maintain memory across multiple sessions (no more "remind me what we were working on")
- Work while you're offline

This is OpenAI's answer to the "agentic" promise that startups like MultiOn, Adept, and others have been chasing. Except OpenAI owns the distribution.

**Why this matters:**

1. **The interface shift is complete.** ChatGPT started as a chat window. Now it's a workforce platform. The conversation metaphor is giving way to the task metaphor. Users won't "chat with AI" -- they'll assign work to it.

2. **Persistent memory changes everything.** Previous AI interactions were stateless by default. You started fresh each time. Workspace Agents remember context, preferences, and ongoing projects. This transforms AI from a tool into a colleague.

3. **Enterprise lock-in accelerates.** Once an agent knows your product feedback, your documentation, your code patterns, and your team's preferences, switching costs skyrocket. OpenAI isn't just selling compute -- it's selling continuity.

4. **The scheduling layer is the real innovation.** Anyone can build a chatbot. Building infrastructure that reliably runs autonomous tasks at scale is hard. OpenAI is betting that reliability and persistence matter more than raw capability.

**The catch:**

Pricing isn't fully transparent yet. OpenAI says these are included in Teams and Enterprise plans "for now." The phrase "for now" should make finance teams nervous. When your AI agents are doing actual work, suddenly usage-based pricing looks very different from $25/seat/month.

**The bottom line:**

Workspace Agents represent the maturation of AI from novelty to infrastructure. This isn't about generating clever email replies anymore. It's about delegating actual cognitive labor. The companies that figure out how to manage, audit, and trust these agents will have a structural advantage. Everyone else will be paying OpenAI to replace their junior staff.
