**The news:** Sundar Pichai announced that 75% of Google's new code is now AI-generated, up from 50% last fall. The company also created a "strike team" to improve its coding models and catch up to Anthropic, whose Claude Code writes 70-90% of its code.

**What actually happened:**

Google is publicly admitting it's behind in the AI coding race. The 75% figure sounds impressive until you realize it's a defensive metric, not a leading one. Anthropic hit similar numbers months ago. Google isn't winning -- it's catching up.

The "strike team" memo from Sergey Brin reportedly demands that "every Gemini engineer must be forced to use internal agents for complex, multistep tasks." Forced adoption is not a sign of product-market fit. It's a sign of internal panic.

**Why this matters:**

1. **Google's AI coding moat is nonexistent.** DeepMind has world-class researchers. Google Cloud has enterprise distribution. But Anthropic's Claude Code has developer love. Google is learning that building great models doesn't automatically mean developers will choose your tools.

2. **The 75% number is misleading.** AI-generated code isn't the same as AI-shipped code. Google has massive internal infrastructure, legacy codebases, and review processes. Most of that 75% requires heavy human modification. The metric measures volume, not value.

3. **Enterprise vs. indie developer split is widening.** Google's Workspace AI updates (Chrome Enterprise AI monitoring, Gmail AI Overviews for business, Meet AI notetaking for in-person meetings) show where its real focus is: selling to IT departments, not winning hacker hearts. Anthropic owns developers. Google owns procurement.

4. **The RAM shortage warning is the buried lede.** Google quietly acknowledged that memory constraints could bottleneck AI development "for years." If the infrastructure layer can't keep up with model demand, the coding race becomes a supply chain war.

**The bottom line:**

Google isn't losing the AI coding war because its models are bad. It's losing because it treated developer tools as an afterthought while Anthropic built a cult. The 75% figure and strike team are damage control, not momentum. In the AI coding race, being big isn't the advantage it used to be.
